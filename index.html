Pilot Hill’s Hidden Gem

98 acres of thriving vines nestled within a gated 850‑acre community on Pilot Hill, California. Our mature vineyard is rooted in sustainability and regenerative agriculture, producing wines of exceptional character.

Our Vineyard

Safari Wineries is a hillside vineyard located at 1,500 feet above sea level. Our average vine age is twenty‑six years, and each planting is thoughtfully spaced (6×10 and 5×6) to maximise sun exposure and airflow. Western‑facing rows capture the afternoon light, while high‑volume wells and soil probes support wise water use.

Mature Vines

Average vine age: 26 years, resulting in complex, balanced fruit.

Optimal Spacing

Plantings spaced 6×10 and 5×6 for ideal canopy management and ripeness.

Hillside Elevation

Fruit grown at 1,500 ft on rolling slopes for natural drainage and cool evenings.

Soil & Vigor

Rocky silt soils of Auburn and Sobrante series moderate vigour for concentrated berries.

Irrigation

High‑volume wells and soil probes deliver measured irrigation exactly when needed.

Vineyard Details

A diverse palette of Bordeaux and Mediterranean varieties thrives in our blocks. Multiple rootstocks and clones create depth and complexity in the fruit.

Varieties

Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Petit Verdot, Vermentino, Sauvignon Blanc, Grenache Blanc, Grenache Gris.

Rootstocks

420A, 1103P, 101‑14, 3309.

Clones

Red clones: 4, 5, 6, 191, 337  •  White clones: FPS1, VCR12.

Commitment to Sustainability

Certified sustainable today and transitioning toward regenerative practices tomorrow. Our farming methods protect the environment and nurture biodiversity for future generations.

Diverse Blocks & Clones

Multiple clones and rootstocks create depth and complexity, allowing us to craft wines with nuance and character.

Quality Control

Pre‑harvest phenolic analysis guides picking decisions for each block, ensuring optimal ripeness and balance.

Optimal Conditions

Ideal elevation, mature vines and carefully managed soils yield small berries packed with flavour and structure.

Regenerative Future

By enhancing soil health, promoting cover crops and encouraging beneficial insects, we are building a resilient ecosystem.
